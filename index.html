Life Around the Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It provides the light and heat that make life on Earth possible. Without the Sun, plants, animals, and humans could not survive.

Scientists study the Sun to better understand how stars work and how solar energy affects our planet. The Suns energy powers weather, ocean currents, and the process of photosynthesis in plants.

Interesting Facts About the Sun

  • The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old.
  • It is made mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • The Sun is about 109 times wider than Earth.
  • Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes to reach Earth.
